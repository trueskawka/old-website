RC - last day
Achievements: * helped with cleaning at RC * visited Eva, Shawn and the baby * bought some gifts and knick-knocks * had a midnight checkout group dinner The never graduation party ended late, so I started my day with a long walk through Manhattan and gift shopping. The weather was perfect for simply sipping my chai latte and talking, taking a ton of photos on the way (some of them are from previous escapades). I finally arrived to RC just in time for the cleaning party - Deniz and Isla had a wonderful idea of cleaning the shared space together. I didn't help as much as I've wanted, since I had to visit Eva and Shawn and hug the baby as the responsible auntie I am. We went for dinner with most of our midnight checkout group and had great time, especially with very intriguing rolled ice cream at a place called Juicy Spot - they literally froze them from milk and other ingredients right before our eyes, in a magical process that included a lot of scraping. I didn't get to say all of my goodbyes the way I've imagined them, but that may be for the best - there were so many feelings and tears in the last few days, I might've not been able to go through more. My time in New York and physical presence at RC is over, but I'm never graduating. 11/05/2016 03:52

RC - never graduation
Achievements: * seen community presentations * had a presentation on open-source contributions * never graduated * played loads of arcade games The last day of my batch started great, with a healthy breakfast with Andy. We got some quality time, chatting away on very serious topic - much too serious for the amount of sleep we've had between ourselves. I got back to RC just in time for presentations: * Leah talked about [neural networks that write](https://github.com/astrieanna/LSTM_for_text), specifically [long short-term memory](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Long_short-term_memory) networks * Andy reminded about his Schwarzenegger's calfs project - [a documentation viewer](https://github.com/aglee/DocSetExplorer) for Apple docsets * V presented his [content tracker in Haskell](https://github.com/vaibhavsagar/duffer), with focus on some of the different uses for his project * Isabel talked about DNA sequences, specifically [cooperative binding](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Cooperative_binding) and [position specific weight matrix](https://www.wikiwand.com/en/Position_weight_matrix) * Brian showed off his cool NBA game visualiser (I'm bummed it's not available online yet) * Trey presented his [first](https://treylawrence.github.io/idk/) p5.js [projects](https://treylawrence.github.io/gray/) I talked about my Outreachy application process, first open-source contribution and how to start contributing to Zulip. I've also had a chance to thank everyone who's helped me with this long and challenging process and mention the Cihan alternative to the rubber duck method - he just sits there and looks disappointed whenever I'm not working. After presentations we were handed our diplomas (Fall 2016 T-shirts) and certificates (an envelope full of niceties from Recursers). There were laughs and some tears, loads of joy and a sense of community. When I saw the Summer 2's graduation six weeks ago, I never imagined it would be such a prominent experience. We then went for delicious burgers and spent a few hours in Barcade, playing arcade games, drinking, laughing and saying our goodbyes. It was a the best of nights.
11/05/2016 03:37

RC - day 80
Achievements: * feelings check-in * wrote niceties for fellow batchers * watched **Before the Flood** * wrote a bit After another all-nighter I got to RC just in time for the last feelings check-in. I expected it to be very emotional, but it turned out to be much more calm and soothing. I don't feel guilty anymore that I'm ready for the next step - gradually I've started feeling more and more like a programmer. I've done very different projects from what I've planned, but still managed to fulfill my goals. A bit sad, grateful and content, I was glad others are also in a good place. Full of feelings after the check-in, I wrote niceties for my fellow batchers. I've decided to start with those I don't know as much and slowly go through the list to my closest friends - with each person it was getting harder and harder to write. It's a happy-sad experience, since all these people mean a lot to me, but at the same time I'm sure I'll never see them again after leaving New York. The best way to approach overflow of feelings was the dollar pizza, with a soda and brownie on top. After this joyous meal, I was unable to do anything apart from writing a little bit of my Twine story and watching [Before the Flood](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=90CkXVF-Q8M) - National Geographic film on climate change.
