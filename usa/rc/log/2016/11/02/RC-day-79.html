RC - day 79

Achievements:

  • had a chat with Rachel and Lyn about NaNoWriMo (inspiration!)
  • got a ton of great comic recommendations
  • had a lot of quality time with my fellow batchers
  • organized my NaNoWriMo experience
  • started my big interactive fiction story
  • worked a bit on Zulip React Native app feature
  • drafted a presentation on first open-source contributions

RC is full of wonders.

First, Harold gave me a book we’ve talked about some time ago - Invisible Cities by Italo Calvino. It was the most unexpected and delightful gift in a long time and I intend to start reading it over my morning chai latte.

Then we’ve had a great talk about NaNoWriMo, writing in general, comic books, regular books, elephants, history, science fiction and everything with Rachel and Lyn. If no other magical thing happened at RC so far, I’d miss it just for this one hour of pure awesomeness. Rachel took notes during our meeting (yes, it was serious business):

  • some of our recent favorite books (The Buried Giant by Kazuo Ishiguro, The Enchantress of Florence by Salman Rushdie, and Hyperion by Dan Simmons)
  • setting personal goals that are independent of NaNoWriMo’s goals because we’re not working on traditionally structured novels
  • we’re going to try to write something daily
  • starting an accountability stream where we check in daily as to whether we’ve met our goals, and weekly with excerpts of what we’ve written, and possibly at the end of the month with some sort of bigger reading/showcase
  • COMICS, LOTS OF COMICS (Pretty Deadly, Habibi, Lazarus, The Wicked + The Divine, Swamp Thing, Fables, Manifest Destiny - I am so behind on comics!)
  • some apps and tools we use for writing (Twine, Scrivener, Ulysses, Google Apps, Scapple, notebooks, Haven, Hemingway)

We’ve also had a fun idea of writing a blog together and I created a simple website to do just that (nothing to show yet). I was so hyped and inspired, that I noted down my goals and ideas and even went as far as to use a mind-map to do a brain-dump. I don’t think I’ve ever written so many messages on Zulip in one evening. And the success for the day was that I actually started my interactive fiction story - at the moment I only have two characters and half of a location, but I’m doing this!

Since our never-graduation is nearing, we’ve also had a warm night of talking and enjoying each others company. It was more important than opening another pull request for Zulip, as the contributions I’m working on at the moment are not time-sensitive and I’ll see my fellow Recursers only for the next two, three, maybe four days if I’m lucky.

Being in the mood for writing, I also drafted a presentation for Thursday on how to start contributing to open-source projects, specifically Zulip. I think it would be great to get some other RC-folks ideas and contributions, since we’re all using the app heavily and will continue using it as community members. And it’s great fun to learn various ideas for improvements.
