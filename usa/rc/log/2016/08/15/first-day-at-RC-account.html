first day at the RC - account

The Recurse Center is a very welcoming and open place I'm going to spend 90% of my time at. Not just because it's what I came here to do, but also because I love it.

I've met a ton of people:

  • Joseph from LA, who's into data science and bioinformatics
  • Isla, a mathematician from Scotland who's also into bioinformatics
  • Paul from Ireland, who has a PhD in compilers and runs his own business
  • Satabdi, a Bengali girl who wants to work on her functional programming
  • Zach, a programmer for RC who advised me on how to deal with cockroaches and actually considers them a NYC thing
  • Michelle, an Australian who loves her JavaScript
  • Leah of Bellevue, who wants to go through a statistics textbook
  • Trey from California, who is in his second batch to have some time for himself between jobs
  • David, who doesn't use his Korean name and wants to build an SMT-solver
  • Lisa, a facilitator from Texas who loves her Android
  • Amy Pickup, whose surname I love, wants to build web apps
  • Vaibhav, an Indonesian guy who's into Haskell
  • Alicia, in love with healthcare and Python
  • Juan of Peru, who wants to play with Erlang and Elixir
  • Michelle, who's done so many things I can't even imagine pair programming with her
  • Ivan, a Russian who wants to experiment with data science, among a ton of other things
  • Miguel, another Haskell enthusiast from Colombia
  • Katherine, a biologist who loves her Python and Vim
  • Lareine from New Jersey, a very shy girl who has a lot to offer, especially with Arduino
  • Leeor from Israel, a lawyer who turned to coding
  • Ivo, a quiet web-dev from Lima
  • James, a backend programmer turned RC facilitator from NY
  • ? from Mid-West, an geologist who does web dev and digs data science
  • Sean, a graduate student in linguistics and language perception
  • Ginger, a mix of a maltese and a poodle (maltipoo), who's just the cutest dog ever

And those are just the people I've talked (or played with in case of Ginger) at least for a few minutes. Altogether I've met about thrice as much.

On one hand, the first day was relaxing. We did introductions, first check-ins, lunch, developer speed-dating, some quiet time and a meetup.

On the other, reiterating my story with new people every few minutes was exhausting. I'm not sure I even know what I've said to most of them. Loads of new ideas, but also insecurity, as I don't know where to start.
