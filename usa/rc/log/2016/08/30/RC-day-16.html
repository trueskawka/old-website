Achievements:

I couldn't sleep today so I got to the RC as soon as possible, since there is nothing much I could do at the apartment. In the morning the trains don't have so many delays and it's also not as hot, so it might be a good longterm strategy.

I finally evaluated how much uninterrupted time it takes before I am productive - breakfast, coffee, reading emails, checking social media and various news channels. I never thought it takes about 1.5 hours, which may explain why I always underestimate my workload. It's not that I need to be more efficient with it, just never thought about it much - after all, it's "just" a few things.

I'm pleased with my level of focus and productivity today. I've read a lot of algorithms books, done some coding, felt like I was going in the right direction to be a better programmer. The only downside was I needed a nap halfway through, as I'm not used to getting up so early. Maybe it's just how I'm supposed to work and I should take it into account over the next few weeks.

I went to the used books store and managed not to buy another book, which is something, seeing as it seems to be an addiction that runs in the family.

The evening was fun and inspiring, as I went to the Hack&&Tell meetup - a series of 5-minute presentations followed by 5-minute Q&A sessions about projects people do in their free time for the pure fun of it. I came up with at least a dozen ideas on what I could do and I'll have to keep myself focused, but it was worth it. The presentations were:

  • virtual reality in JavaScript - WebVR
  • automatisation of Twitter posts for the Turing incomplete podcast
  • a new programming language called Zig
  • useful and tiny website ismytrainfucked.com
  • a game with Google ngrams dataset "Word Freq"
  • an app for buostrofying text
  • a JavaScript application of spreadsheets called spread.js
  • visualisation of NYC violations by precinct
  • simulation of the way colourblind see the world, to help designers create better products
  • Descartes - CSS written in JS

I don't think I'll be able to not do any side projects, as I won a book - Kinect Hacks - Tips & Tools for Motion and Pattern Detection. I'm having some wild ideas about working with videos of Ginger.
