RC - day 44

Achievements:

  • stopped forcing myself to be a morning person
  • warm-up in Python
  • took a few steps to build my Markdown app
  • improved my Gmail experience
  • paired on code dojo problem with Cihan
  • took part in the improv night, a fun and amazing experience (all thanks to Sarah!)
  • had a wonderful evening with RC friends

I think it was high time to stop kidding myself - I am not nor will I ever be a morning person. Starting the day slowly and getting to RC just in time for my breakfast and 1pm check-in was the perfect solution to my motivation problems. Firstly, I got so much sleep I woke up without an alarm and wasn’t stressed about being late. Coming in during the day, seeing all those people working away on their laptops, focused, calm and going forward with their plans was my driving force for today.

Thanks to Mike, warmups are alive and well again, and I’ve had a nice half hour playing with a simple problem in Python.

I then proceeded to plan my Markdown app - the features I want to include, people to ask for help and pair with, general look and feel of it. I’ve asked Marcio for some help on getting started and will hopefully pair soon with Nick (macOS development), Andy (macOS development and Markdown) and Michelle (Markdown filtering). Can’t wait!

As for minor tweaks, I’ve spent a productive half-hour learning all the Gmail shortcuts. I have so many messages coming in every day, it’s surprising I’ve never done that before.

Cihan made it in time for code dojo and we’ve created together a working simple solution in Python and then struggled to reimplement it in Elm. About an hour after the dojo ended, a simplistic approach got implemented. I want to work more on my functional programming, since doing even the simplest things is such a struggle. It’s like relearning to ride a bike.

The improvisation night was so amazing it’s hard to describe. Sarah is such a wonderful teacher! We’ve had a lot of group and individual exercises, all of them focused on supporting each other no matter what. Improv is really about saying “yes and” to everything, I want to do more of that in programming. And a lot more improv. It could’ve also been the group - it’s not often you come up with a new theory for global warming: flying unicorns pooping ice-cream into the atmosphere (that was a very inside joke, no worries if it wasn’t clear).

After the improv I went for great (and very cheap!) vegetarian food with some of my RC friends. It was firstly about bidding farewell to Naomi, but mostly it was simply an amazing evening.

Lastly, some food for thought:

  • write it anyway
  • how to politely deal with interruptions
  • why smart people are expected to be stupid at work
  • are you a jerk?

I seem overly amazingly and wonderfully happy recently, I should work on my adjectives starting tomorrow.
