RC - day 39

Achievements:

  • worked with Jeni, Alicia and Satabdi on the sexism tracking app
  • Abstract Salad Factory
  • MCed presentations
  • had fun at the farewell picnic
  • walked through the Brooklyn Bridge

The productive part of the day was really short, since today we had a farewell picnic for the Summer 2 batch (who overlapped with us) and some of our fellow batchers from Fall 1, who came here only for six weeks.

I’ve managed to get some coding started on our sexism tracking app, pairing with Jeni on views. The Abstract Salad Factory helped me then with not feeling guilty about indulging myself during the presentations and the picnic.

I volunteered to MC the presentations, i.e. being the master of ceremonies - introducing speakers and making sure everything works out. It was fun, albeit a bit stressful. I had to come up to every person that was presenting, interrupt their last touchups and make sure their laptops work with the projector, the loudspeakers won’t make everybody’s ears bleed and they have whatever they might need. Even though I’ve had a few missteps on the way, everything ran pretty smoothly.

Presentations themselves were hilarious, some of my favourites were:

  • Lareine’s implementation of ethical engineering algorithm, with some surprising turns on who “deserves” to live - here are some of her thoughts from the previous presentation
  • Jesse’s DJ app, on which he worked together with Taylor - it automatically mixes music, just as a DJ would
  • Michelle’s intranationalization engine for Markdown, which she hilariously presented with examples of possible uses of filtering words in text (e.g. you might want your resume to be “tech” for some people, but “fun” for others)
  • Juan’s CHIP-8 emulator, which he got to play pong!

We went to the Brooklyn Bridge park and had a few wonderful hours in the sun, eating snacks and playing some outdoor sports. I learned to throw the egghand (Americans would call it football), but I need to work on not getting hit in the face with it. Also, a squirrel was very into our cookies and she was very impatient.

I’ve also tried an American burger for the very first time and the experience was a bit disturbing - it tasted like something twice deep-fried. Striving to have more NYC experiences, I went on a walk through Brooklyn Bridge, or rather was dragged through it with the help of Cihan and Joe. It is ver high and the sidewalk is made of wood, my fear of heights won’t let me forget about it.
