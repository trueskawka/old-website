Dialogue

A plan is a plan

I’ve decided to relearn Arabic this year, as I’ve completely forgotten the language and I regret it every day. Since I haven’t done systematic learning in a while, I need to follow some steps and have a plan.

The goal

To understand intermediate level conversations in Arabic - be able to watch tv series and follow the plot, listen to the news, ask for help.

To talk about my passions and work.

To read books in Arabic.

To communicate with a native speaker.

What I’ve done so far

  • I read a few articles on TextFugu - a book for Japanese self-learners - mostly on how to get started and motivate myself

  • I noted down my reasons and motivations along with my fears

  • I created my first Anki deck for software-related terms

  • I organized my many Arabic learning books

  • I took my Arabic keyboard cover and put it on my laptop

  • I’ve created this dedicated blog

  • I’ve done some Google searching on Arabic resources, self-learning, motivation and touch-typing

The Plan

Minimum

  • Learn Arabic every day for 52 minutes:
    • Do one lesson from any of my many resources
    • Create an Anki deck with the words
    • Write a blog post for my learning log

  • Once a week watch a short video in Arabic

  • Once a week listen to a podcast/audiobook in Arabic

  • Practice touch-typing an hour a week (can be 10-minute bursts)

  • Go through Anki decks during coding idle time

Additional ideas

  • Read a chapter of a book in Arabic

  • Translate a short article/poem to or from Arabic

  • Draw a detailed picture with annotations for my own picture dictionary

  • Try to speak/write Arabic in a real-life setting

  • Translate song lyrics

  • Find a new resources
