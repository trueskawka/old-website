10/28/2016 04:30

RC - day 74

Achievements:

  • one-on-ones with Jennifer and James
  • pair presentation with Chris
  • job fair
  • extracted another part of Zulip CSS
  • played with Twine

I’ve started the day just right by having a wonderful one-on-one chat with Jennifer - a new experiment at RC that helps us get to know each other better. I admire her sense of direction and focus, and also love how Recursers share the need for growing and learning hard things.

V came up with a great idea of having pair presentations to talk about awesome projects some Recursers haven’t presented yet. I’ve had a chance to pair with Chris on his Citibike Routing App - a learning exercise in Angular 2 and Google Maps APIs. We’ve also discussed some possible tweaks and pairing opportunities based on the app. He was so passionate while explaining the “mid-end” concept of a backend on the front-end that I might just give Angular 2 another try.

Other awesome pairings for today were:

  • Harold helped Andrea with presenting her style transfer app for images
  • Michelle talked about Andrew’s tools for making algorithmic art easier
  • Mike explained neural networks after a crash course from Deniz
  • Trey personalized Hacker News and presented it with V
  • Isabel and Isla used Bokeh to visualize genomic data

In the evening there was another job fair at RC. I wasn’t particularly interested in any of the companies, as they didn’t have remote opportunities, so I wanted to focus on helping other Recursers. There was nothing much for me to do, as most of them were engaged in conversations without any encouragement, so I turned to half-mingling, half-enjoying some unfocused time.

I am getting good at splitting the Zulip CSS. I’ve moved yet another large chunk of it to a new file and I can now plan more than a few steps ahead, understanding better how the refactoring could look and what other logical splits would be helpful. What started out as a tedious, but necessary, task became a great learning opportunity. It also supports other developers on the team, so I feel helpful while learning.
