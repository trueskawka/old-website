RC - day 13

Achievements:

  • self check-in
  • keeping the meditation habit streak
  • warm-ups in Ruby
  • first board-game design session with Tanoy
  • failed shopping turned into a successful trip

Today I've learned how post-it notes and a few hours can be used for an awesome afternoon. I've finally had my first board-game design session with Tanoy.

The game theme is sailing in a small boat through open and dangerous seas. First we did some brainstorming, while munching cookies, and after we've been happy with the initial ideas we got down to prototyping. Making the tiny boats, board and all kinds of tokens made it suddenly very real.

The main takeout from today is that we need to play the game and adjust, rather than just talk about concepts and get more and more excited. Next session will probably happen next week and, if everything goes well, we'll soon create prototypes to play them with other RCers.

This game is supposed to be mainly a case study for game design, so I can learn to do it better. But if something cute and fun happens on the way, then it's even more worth it.

As for the failed shopping - I wanted to buy shoes, as I've managed to break a pair during yesterday's karaoke. I ended up getting a big mug and two books.

08/27/2016 21:34

RC - day 12

Achievements:

  • self check-in
  • keeping the meditation habit streak
  • warm-ups in Ruby
  • refactoring dojo
  • algorithms group meeting
  • helping Jade with Google Analytics for his website
  • coding jeopardy
  • karaoke

Today was mostly about bonding.

After starting the day right with meditation, pairing with Vaibhav and Isla during the refactoring dojo was a wonderful experience. We did the Tennis Refactoring Kata in Python - worked together on purposely bad code in a way that didn't break the tests, but helped with understanding the underlying logic of the program.

Later we planned for a short meeting of the algorithms course group and ended up spending more than an hour together, crunching problem sets.

After a quick mini-session on Google Analytics with Jade, I went out for late lunch with a bunch of people, to also spend some time with Emil on his last day of residency at RC. After we got back, we've had an exciting code jeopardy (which Vaibhav ruled, I think he must've devoured Wikipedia). Short chat about karaoke revealed an existing RC karaoke app and that's when we found the box labeled "karaoke". A few hours later, extremely tired and very happy we went our separate ways, to get some sleep after an awesome night.

As you can see, the setup was professional - we were using a projector, two mics, a disco-ball and a big chunk of the floor to dance while singing.
