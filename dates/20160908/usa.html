09/08/2016 22:50

RC - day 25

Achievements:

  • check-in
  • keeping the meditation habit streak
  • warm-up in Python
  • attended Lisa’s workshop on how to read code
  • attended Emily’s workshop on p5.js
  • had a presentation on side projects
  • wrote Airbnb about switching apartments

I guess there comes a point when enough is enough. Tonight the subway took record time to get from Brooklyn to Uptown - more than 2 hours. For about an hour the train was just standing in the tunnel and waiting, without much information why and when will we start moving again. Dead tired and disappointed, I got to the apartment and decided to switch as soon as possible.

Apart from the local transport incidents, the day was great. I attended a workshop on how to read code first thing in the morning, had a check-in, then a long nap (it’s sometimes much better to simply let it go for a moment). I then contacted Airbnb and mostly worked on my presentation, learning more about Jekyll plugins in the meantime.

The presentations were wonderful, I couldn’t decide on just three:

  • Andrey’s birthday over IP app was wonderful, I will be sure to use it while in the US
  • Ivan’s live coded implementation of a Brainfuck REPL in five minutes was jaw-dropping
  • Andy’s talk on his chess playing app was hilarious, my quote of the day is programmers use code to conquer personal shortcomings - he is yet another person who should do developer stand-up
  • Harold had a great presentation on word embedding, I will always remember that most_similar(vectors['paris'] - vectors['france'] + vectors['poland']) equals Warsaw

At the end of the day, Emily introduced us to p5.js and my first app will hopefully be finished by the end of the day tomorrow. I loved her work and the way she conducted the workshop.

09/08/2016 16:00

RC - day 24

Achievements:

  • remote check-in (I was late)
  • keeping the meditation habit streak
  • warm-up in Ruby
  • writing my first app in Swift thanks to Jesse’s workshop
  • pairing with Aissam on WebRTC
  • worked on presentation
  • went to Drunk TED Talks

Living in the Uptown New York has some ups and downs, sadly mostly downs - I was late for my check-in, as going back to the apartment yesterday took me twice as long as usual. I should probably not use the subway after 11pm and lower my expectations. I’ll just think about it as a suburban train rather than what we consider a subway in Europe.

Jesse was kind enough to do another round of his Swift workshop and I’ve built my first iOS app - a simple implementation of Lights Out. It was a fun afternoon activity.

I’ve randomly started talking to Aissam about his current work on WebRTC - a technology that allows streaming video and audio through a simple implementation in the browser. We paired on a Snapchat-like feature I hope he’ll present some time soon.

In the evening we went for Drunk TED Talks on conspiracies. With something advertised as “TED talks” I expected it to be a bit different - the presentations weren’t TED-like, but some presenters did look drunk. There were four conspiracy areas:

  • Nicolas Cage
  • One Direction
  • Blood Cults
  • Jill Stein (Green Party candidate for the US president)

I never thought I’d say that, but the most interesting presentation was on One Direction. There was also a Polish accent I didn’t expect - apparently there is an XVIII-century painting by Karol de Prevot in Sandomierz’s cathedral, allegedly depicting ritual murder of Christians by Jews.

Afterwards we went for drinks to a very nice pub. I’ve tried some seaweed cider, but opted for a regular apple one - the salty taste and seaweed smell weren’t really appealing.
