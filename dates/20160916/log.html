RC - day 32

Achievements:

  • sleeping in to destress
  • meditating
  • reading up for Friday interview prep
  • had a presentation on learning new skills

Thursday was a presentation day. We’ve had 8 usual evening presentations and 12 non-technical talks, amounting to three hours of learning something new every 5 to 10 minutes.

Three technical talks I enjoyed most were:

  • Satabdi’s Noteranger app for searching her notes
  • Andrea’s great idea for visualising art by style (I’d love to learn more about convolutional neural networks)
  • Marf’s coverage of her two-week spree of exclusively pairing made me want to pair more

The non-technical talks were so diverse it’s really hard to pick favourites, but some of them resonated with me:

  • Alicia’s talk on media literacy and porn consumption was marvelous, I hope she shares her thesis with us (I’ve only managed to find the title Media Literacy for Pornography: Assessing the Impact of an Experimental Curriculum)
  • Sarah’s thoughts on improv inspired me to try it - hope to do it at RC soon
  • Juliano’s description on physiology of singing was cut short, would love to hear more about it (workshop anyone?)
  • Isabel’s presentation on genome editing with CRISPR brought some hopes for the H+ enthusiast in me
  • Taylor guided us rapidly through literary theory
  • James shared some insights on jazz reharmonisation, which makes me want to learn to play guitar properly even more

I’ve had my own non-techical moment on The First 20 Hours book and I’m hoping on writing a proper blog post on it soon and start my first learning experiment next week.

09/16/2016 13:36

RC - day 31

Achievements:

  • taking some time off
  • asking for help when I needed it

Everything is great now.

Wednesday I had a health scare - I fainted on the subway and ended up in a hospital. Nothing much happened, apart from lightly hitting my head on the subway car.

The experience was really stressful because of the insurance. Instead of keeping my cool and being happy someone takes care of me, I was constantly trying to calculate if my travel insurance is going to cover it. That’s a very US experience.

What surprised me is that you get the bill for emergency care about 4-6 weeks after the incident and you actually get three bills:

  • one from the ambulance company, because the ambulances are privately owned
  • one from the hospital
  • one from the doctor that checks you up at the hospital

Altogether an ambulance ride, an ECG & a blood pressure check at the hospital and a two minute chat with the doctor can cost anything between $2.000 and $8.000. Good my insurance is for more than $30.000.

With the Airbnb problems and lack of productivity over the weekend, I felt lost and extremely stressed. Luckily, my loved ones and the RC community were there for me. I decided to take it slow.
