10/16/2016 06:25

RC - day 61

Achievements:

  • algorithmic interview prep
  • one-on-one with James
  • improv games
  • solving an infinite matrix problem

After another all-nighter I should just start calling them ‘nights’, since I can’t seem to be able to fall asleep. Got to the apartment in the morning, slept a few hours, got back to RC and went to interview prep, which was loads of fun. I’ve asked Dan a fun brain-teaser questions I love to ask people and got one from Chris that was very satisfying.

A one-on-one with James was very helpful, since with the open format of RC it’s sometimes challenging to keep your priorities and goals in mind. After a fruitful chat, I got a ton of positive energy from improv games with Sarah and a group of wonderful Recursers.

Another night full of learning, talking and coding followed, with the brain-teaser on an infinite matrix taking a lot of Andy’s and my time. If you want to try it, here is the problem statement:

    Imagine an infinite 2-dimensional matrix containing the
    numbers 1, 2, 3, ... arranged in a diagonally winding order
    as below. Write an algorithm that returns the matrix element
    at row i and column j.

    1    3    4    10   11   21   22   36   37   55
    2    5    9    12   20   23   35   38   54   57
    6    8    13   19   24   34   39   53   58   76
    7    14   18   25   33   40   52   59   75   82
    15   17   26   32   41   51   60   74   83  101
    16   27   31   42   50   61   73   84  100  111
    28   30   43   49   62   72   85   99  112  130
    29   44   48   63   71   86   98  113  129  144
    45   47   64   70   87   97  114  128  145  163
    46   65   69   88   96  115  127  146  162  181

10/16/2016 06:11

RC - day 60

Achievements:

  • watched community presentations
  • went for the Etsy Code as Craft meet-up

As much fun as all-nighters are, they make it very challenging to be productive on presentation days. After getting some sleep at the apartment, I got to RC at 3:15pm, which was in perfect sync with snacks before presentations.

Some of the highlights:

  • Marko had a great presentation about inducing hallucinations in neural networks, mainly deep dreaming on audio spectrograms
  • Andrea presented his NES emulator and reminded me of Kirby - one of my favourite childhood games for Game Boy
  • Tara talked about serverless architecture

After presentations we went for a meet-up at Etsy, which was about mingling, great food and more presentations:

  • Leah presented the Haiku OS Vector Icon Format
  • Keyur talked about the data structures transformations at Etsy
  • Emily unveiled her personal journey to creative coding, approached with a tinkering mindset
  • Stefanie focused on API-first infrastructure at Etsy

One of the best things about the night was a photo booth, which we used extensively for group photos.
