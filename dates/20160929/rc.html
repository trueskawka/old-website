I've reached out on Zulip to Sumana (experienced Outreachy mentor) and the thread helped me tremendously, especially when Alice shared her application experiences. I'll focus on trying out the projects I'm interested in locally (or reading up about the ones that are not strictly technical), maybe even make small contributions over the weekend. Instead of simply procrastinating, I've managed to focus on science behind making decisions and quitting, inspired by an [article on optimal stopping](https://medium.com/galleys/optimal-stopping-45c54da6d8d0#.txu450qhe). I have some slides for tomorrow, but I'm not yet sure if I'm going to present - it's not entirely a technical topic. Though knowing when to quit a project seems like a useful skill for Recursers. The feelings check-in was a blast. It's interesting how hearing about insecurities of others helps with my own struggles. I visited the always warm and welcoming Eva and Shawn. The baby is going to be born soon, can't wait! Also, I might give back to my American family and help out with some web development in exchange for dinner and cheesecake. Friday is going to be a busy day: * interview prep - I've been a bit late for prep signups, so instead of algorithms I'm just going to do regular interview questions * watercolours - I'll spend some time with Nayley on the pure pleasure of playing with paints * blog club - we're starting a new group at RC, encouraging each other to write more and/or better blog posts
